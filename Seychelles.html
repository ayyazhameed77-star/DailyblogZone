Aerial view of the stunning Seychelles islands

Vue aeriel bote zil Sesel

The Republic of Seychelles is an archipelagic island country in the Indian Ocean, off the eastern coast of Africa. Comprising 115 islands, it is home to some of the world's most beautiful beaches, rare wildlife, and a vibrant Creole culture. With a population of approximately 100,000 people, it is one of the smallest countries in Africa but boasts the highest GDP per capita on the continent.

Repiblik Sesel se en peyi zil dan Losean Endyen, kot larivaz les Lafrik. Li konsist 115 zil e li ena bann plaz ki pli bel dan lemond, bann zannimo raar, ek en kiltir Kreol vibran. Avek en popilasion environ 100,000 moun, li se enn dan bann peyi ki pli piti dan Lafrik, me li ena GDP par kapit ki pli o dan kontinan.

📜 History of Seychelles

📜 Listwar Sesel

The history of Seychelles is a fascinating tale of discovery, colonization, and cultural fusion. First sighted by Arab traders and later by Portuguese explorers, the islands remained uninhabited until the mid-18th century.

Listwar Sesel se en zistwar pasionan de dekouver, kolonizasion, ek fuzion kiltirel. Bann komersan Arab ek bann explorater Portigee ti trouv zil la premie fwa, me zil la ti rete san moun ziska milye 18em siek.

1756

French Captain Corneille Nicholas Morphey claims the islands for France, naming them after French Finance Minister Jean Moreau de Séchelles. The "Stone of Possession" is laid on Mahé.

Kapiten franse Corneille Nicholas Morphey pran zil la pou Fras, li don zil non Ministre Finans Jean Moreau de Séchelles. "Pierre de Possession" pose lor Mahé.

1770

First permanent settlement established by French settlers from Mauritius, bringing African slaves to work on plantations.

Premie koloni permanan etabli par bann kolon franse ki sorti Moris, ki amenn bann esklav Afriken pou travay lor plantasion.

1794

British forces seize control during the Napoleonic Wars, though French influence remains strong.

Fors Britanik pran kontrol pandan Lagerr Napoleon, me enfluans franse rete for.

1814

Under the Treaty of Paris, Seychelles officially becomes a British colony, administered from Mauritius.

Anba Traite Paris, Sesel vin en koloni Britanik ofisielman, administre depi Moris.

1835

Abolition of slavery transforms the social and economic structure. Freed slaves and later Indian and Chinese laborers shape the Creole identity.

Abolision lesklavaz transforme striktir sosyal ek ekonomik. Bann esklav libere ek bann travayer Endien ek Sinwa ki vini plitar forme lidentite Kreol.

1841

Port Royal is renamed Victoria in honor of Queen Victoria, becoming the capital.

Port Royal gagn non Victoria an loner Rezin Victoria, ek li vin kapital.

1903

Seychelles becomes a separate Crown Colony. The iconic Clock Tower (a miniature of London's Big Ben) is erected in Victoria.

Sesel vin en Crown Colony separer. Lorloz ikonik (en ti kopie Big Ben Londre) monte dan Victoria.

1976

Independence! Seychelles becomes a sovereign republic within the Commonwealth, with James Mancham as first President.

Endepandans! Sesel vin en repiblik souveren dan Commonwealth, avek James Mancham kouma premie Prezidan.

1977

France-Albert René comes to power, leading the country for 27 years and implementing major social reforms.

France-Albert René pran pouvwar, li dirige peyi pou 27 lanner ek met an plas bann reform sosyal majer.

🏙️ Victoria — The Capital City

🏙️ Victoria — Kapital Sesel

The iconic Clock Tower in the heart of Victoria

Lorloz ikonik dan ler Victoria

Victoria, located on Mahé Island, is one of the world's smallest capitals, yet it pulses with energy and charm. Founded by the French in 1778 as "L'Établissement" and later renamed by the British, this city is a living museum of colonial architecture and Creole vibrancy.

Victoria, ki sitire lor zil Mahé, se ennan bann kapital ki pli piti dan lemond, me li plen lenerzi ek charme. Fonde par Franse an 1778 kouma "L'Établissement" ek plitar renomme par Britanik, sa vil la se en mize viv de larkitektir kolonial ek vibrans Kreol.

Top Attractions

Bann Attraksion Prensipal

The bustling Sir Selwyn Selwyn-Clarke Market

Marke Sir Selwyn Selwyn-Clarke bien okipe

Sir Selwyn Selwyn-Clarke Market: Built in 1840, this is the beating heart of Victoria. Under the shade of a giant mango tree, vendors sell fresh fish, tropical fruits (mango, papaya, passion fruit), spices (vanilla, cinnamon), and handmade crafts. The colors, aromas, and friendly banter make it an unforgettable experience.

Marke Sir Selwyn Selwyn-Clarke: Bati an 1840, sa se ler Victoria. Anba lonbraz en gros pie mango, bann vander vann pwason fre, bann fri tropikal (mango, papay, frwi pasion), bann epis (vanil, kanmel), ek bann lartizanat. Bann kouler, loder, ek bann konversasion amikal fer li en eksperians inoubliab.

Cathedral of Our Lady of Immaculate Conception: Built in 1874 and renovated in the 1990s, this is the largest Catholic church in Seychelles. Its stained-glass windows and beautiful gardens offer a peaceful retreat.

Katedral Notre Dame de l'Immaculée Conception: Bati an 1874 ek renove dan bann anne 1990, sa se legliz katolik ki pli gran dan Sesel. So bann lafenetr vitrail ek boli zarden ofer en retret pe.

Arul Mihu Navasakthi Vinayagar Temple: This brightly colored Hindu temple, built in 1992, represents the harmonious multicultural fabric of Seychelles. The ornate roof is a photographer's delight.

Temple Arul Mihu Navasakthi Vinayagar: Sa temple Indou bien kouler, bati an 1992, reprezant tissi multikiltirel harmonie Sesel. Toit la bien orne, en plezir pou fotografer.

The colorful Hindu Temple in Victoria

Temple Indou kouler dan Victoria

National Botanical Gardens: Founded in 1901, these gardens span 10,000 square meters and house over 500 plant species, including the legendary Coco de Mer palm, 40 species of palms, and an orchid garden with 150 varieties. Giant Aldabra tortoises roam freely here!

Zarden Botanik Nasyonal: Fonde an 1901, bann zarden sa fer 10,000 metre kare ek konten plis ki 500 espes plant, ki enkli pie palm Coco de Mer lejenda, 40 espes palm, ek en zarden orkide avek 150 variete. Bann torti Aldabra geant promen lib dan sa!

🗣️ Culture & Seychellois Creole

🗣️ Kiltir ek Kreol Seselwa

Seychellois culture is a rich Creole melting pot — a blend of French, African, Indian, Chinese, and British influences. This fusion is evident in the cuisine (octopus curry, grilled fish with Creole sauce, ladob dessert), music (Sega and Moutya rhythms), and daily life.

Kiltir Seselwa se en melting pot Kreol rich — en melanz de Franse, Afriken, Endien, Sinwa, ek Britanik. Sa fuzion la vizib dan kizin (kari zourit, pwason griyav ek sos Kreol, ladob), mizik (ritm Sega ek Moutya), ek lavi toulejour.

Seychellois Creole (Kreol Seselwa)

Kreol Seselwa

Kreol is the mother tongue of 99% of Seychellois people and one of the three official languages. It is a French-based creole with simplified grammar, enriched by African, English, Hindi, and Malagasy words. Since independence in 1976, the government has promoted Kreol through education and media. The Creole Institute of Seychelles (Lenstiti Kreol), established in 1981, standardizes the language's spelling and grammar.

Kreol se lang maternel 99% bann Seselwa ek ennan trwa lang ofisiel. Li se en kreol base lor franse avek gramere sinplifie, enrichi par bann mo Afriken, Angle, Hindi, ek Malgas. Depi endepandans an 1976, gouvenman promouv Kreol atrav ledikasion ek media. Lenstiti Kreol Sesel, ki etabli an 1981, standarize lortograf ek gramere lang la.

Best Time to Visit

Meyer Ler pou Vizite

The ideal time is during the southeast trade winds (May to November) when temperatures are milder (24-29°C) and humidity is lower. March and April are the hottest months, but perfect for diving.

Ler meyer se pandan aliz lesid (Me pou Novem) kan tanperer pli dou (24-29°C) ek imidite pli ba. Mars ek Avril se bann mwa ki pli so, me parfe pou plonze.

Must-Visit Islands

Zil ki Bizin Vizite

Mahé: The largest island, home to Victoria and Morne Seychellois National Park.
Praslin: Famous for Vallée de Mai, a UNESCO site with Coco de Mer palms.
La Digue: Known for Anse Source d'Argent, one of the world's most photographed beaches.
Aldabra: The world's second-largest coral atoll, home to giant tortoises.

Mahé: Zil ki pli gran, kot Victoria ek Parke Nasyonal Morne Seychellois.
Praslin: Koni pou Vallée de Mai, en sit UNESCO avek bann palm Coco de Mer.
La Digue: Koni pou Anse Source d'Argent, ennan bann plaz ki pli fotografye dan lemond.
Aldabra: Deziem pli gran atoll koray dan lemond, kot bann torti geant viv.

Practical Tips

Bann Konsey Pratik

• Currency: Seychellois Rupee (SCR), but Euros and USD are widely accepted.
• Driving: Left-hand side. Car rental and buses are available.
• Visa: No visa required for most nationalities; a Visitor's Permit is issued on arrival.
• Nature: Please respect the delicate ecosystem. Do not touch tortoises or pick shells.

• Larzan: Roupie Seselwa (SCR), me Euro ek USD aksepte partou.
• Konduir: Kot les. Louer loto ek bis disponib.
• Visa: Nena bezwen visa pou plispar nasyonalite; en Permi Viziter donn ler arive.
• Nature: Respek lekosistem delicat. Touche torti ou pran kokiy pa autorize.

🏳️ The Flag of Seychelles

🏳️ Drapo Sesel

The five-colored bands of the Seychelles flag

Bann senn kouler drapo Sesel

Adopted in 1996, the flag features five oblique bands radiating from the lower hoist corner:
Blue — the sky and the sea surrounding Seychelles
Yellow — the sun giving light and life
Red — the people and their determination to work in unity and love
White — social justice and harmony
Green — the land and natural environment

Adopte an 1996, drapo la montre senn bann senn oblik ki sorti depi koin anba goch:
Ble — lesiel ek lanmer ki antour Sesel
Jon — soley ki donn limyer ek lavi
Rouz — bann moun ek zeterminasion pou travay dan linite ek lamour
Blan — lasite sosyal ek harmonie
Ver — later ek anvironman natirel
