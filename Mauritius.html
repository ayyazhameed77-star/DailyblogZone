About Mauritius

National Flag of the Republic of Mauritius

The Four Stripes of Identity

Adopted on March 12, 1968 — the day of independence — the flag of Mauritius carries the soul of a nation in four bold horizontal bands. Each color whispers a chapter of the island's story.

Red

The blood of freedom fighters and the struggle for independence.

Blue

The Indian Ocean that cradles the island and connects it to the world.

Yellow

The golden light of a new dawn — hope for the future.

Green

The lush vegetation and agricultural heritage of the island.

Famous Cities & Places

Port Louis

The vibrant capital where colonial architecture meets bustling markets. Walk through the Central Market's spice-scented alleys, explore the Caudan Waterfront, and visit the UNESCO-listed Aapravasi Ghat — the landing point of indentured laborers who shaped the island's multicultural soul.

Capital Heritage

Grand Baie

The "Tropical Riviera" of Mauritius. Crystal-clear lagoons, powdery white sand, and a lively coastal village atmosphere. Perfect for snorkeling, sunset catamaran cruises, and beachfront dining under the stars.

Beach Nature

Chamarel

Home to the legendary Seven Coloured Earths — a surreal landscape of sand dunes in red, brown, violet, and gold. Nearby, the 95-meter Chamarel Waterfall cascades into a lush valley, and the Rhumerie de Chamarel offers world-class rum tastings.

Nature Wonder Heritage

Le Morne

A UNESCO World Heritage site and a powerful symbol of freedom. The 556-meter basaltic mountain was a refuge for escaped slaves. Today, it offers breathtaking hikes, kitesurfing at "One Eye" wave, and the famous underwater waterfall illusion visible from above.

Mountain UNESCO Site

Flic en Flac

An 8-kilometer stretch of pristine beach on the west coast, renowned as the island's best diving destination. Explore underwater cathedrals, encounter dolphins at dawn, and unwind at beach shacks serving freshly caught seafood.

Beach Diving

Belle Mare

A 10-kilometer paradise of blindingly white sand and turquoise waters on the east coast. Luxury resorts line the shore, while the coral reef teems with marine life. A sunrise here is an experience you'll carry forever.

Travel Tips

📅

Best Time to Visit

May to December for dry, pleasant weather. Avoid January–March (cyclone season).

🍛

Must-Try Food

Dholl puri, boulettes, mine frite, fresh seafood curry, and local rum.

🚗

Getting Around

Rent a car for freedom, or use taxis and buses for main routes.

💰

Currency

Mauritian Rupee (MUR). Credit cards accepted at most hotels and restaurants.
